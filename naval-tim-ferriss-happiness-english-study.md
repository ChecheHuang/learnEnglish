## 1. 歡迎回到節目：為何 Naval 不再上別人的 Podcast

### 逐句對照

**主持人:** Naval, welcome back to the show.
Naval，歡迎回到節目。

**Naval:** Yeah, thanks for having me.
好的，謝謝你找我來。

**主持人:** If I recall correctly, I was crushed in podcast of the year by Jamie Foxx. So I need weaker competition this time around. If you could just tone it down this year, please.
如果我沒記錯，我在「年度 Podcast」被 Jamie Foxx 打得慘敗。所以這次我需要弱一點的對手。今年就麻煩你收斂一點，拜託了。

**Naval:** I will do my best. Yeah, Jamie Foxx is the consummate performer, so he is formidable competition on a podcast. But you are a different breed.
我盡力。是啊，Jamie Foxx 是頂尖的表演者，所以在 Podcast 上他是很難纏的對手。不過你是另一種類型的人。

**主持人:** And I understand that you get asked to do podcasts a lot. So what type of disclaimer would you like to give?
我知道有很多人邀你上 Podcast。那你想先做什麼樣的聲明嗎？

**Naval:** Yeah, I don't go on other people's podcasts anymore. I had to make an exception for you because you're the OG. You put me on the map, you're a good friend, and frankly I wouldn't even be known if it weren't for you. So I definitely have to thank you and honor your request to have me back.
對，我現在不上別人的 Podcast 了。我必須為你破例，因為你是元老級的人物。是你讓我打開知名度，你是我的好朋友，老實說要不是你，我根本不會有名。所以我一定要謝謝你，並答應你請我回來的邀約。

**Naval:** I generally don't do podcasts anymore, or at least other people's podcasts, for a couple of reasons. First is I just don't believe in sequels. I think sequels suck.
我基本上不再上 Podcast 了，至少不上別人的，原因有幾個。第一，我就是不相信續集這種東西。我覺得續集都很爛。

**Naval:** You look at sequels in movies and whatnot, and they're usually pretty bad. They cost three times as much, they're long-winded, the person now has an ego and a reputation to defend. They've already said what they were gonna say, so they're just repeating themselves to some level.
你看看電影之類的續集，通常都滿糟的。成本貴上三倍、又臭又長，而且那個人現在有了自尊和名聲要維護。他們該說的都已經說完了，所以某種程度上只是在重複自己講過的話。

**Naval:** Maybe the first run they took an idea they've had for a decade or three decades and they expounded on it and it sounded great. And then in the sequel, whether it's a book or a movie, they have to come up with something equally good, but they only have two years to do it, and it's just really difficult to do.
也許第一次，他們把一個醞釀了十年、甚至三十年的想法拿出來詳加闡述，聽起來很棒。可是到了續集，不管是書還是電影，他們得端出一樣好的東西，卻只有兩年的時間，這真的很難辦到。

**Naval:** So I think sequels just make everybody look bad, and I'm not a fan of sequels. Yet here we are.
所以我覺得續集只會讓大家都難看，我不喜歡續集。然而我們還是坐在這裡了。

**主持人:** The thing about sequels, they make money, people want them, people crave them because there's a certain familiarity to it. But hopefully we won't embarrass ourselves too much.
續集的重點是，它們賺錢、大家想看、大家渴望它，因為有一種熟悉感。不過但願我們不會太丟臉。

**Naval:** So yes, I don't do sequels. We'll make an exception here and kind of see how it goes. Just don't have Jamie Foxx back on, at least not this year.
所以沒錯，我不拍續集。這次我們破個例，看看效果如何。只要別再請 Jamie Foxx 回來就好，至少今年別。

### 💬 慣用語 & 口語解析

- **`put me on the map`**：讓某人／某地「打開知名度、變得重要」。
- **`the OG`**：original gangster 的縮寫，口語指「元老、開山始祖、原創者」，帶敬意。
- **`a different breed`**：「另一種類型的人」，指與眾不同、自成一格。
- **`tone it down`**：「收斂一點、降低強度」。
- **`sequels suck`**：`suck` 是很口語的「很爛、很糟」。

### 📝 文法 / 句型註記

- **`I wouldn't even be known if it weren't for you.`** — 與現在事實相反的假設語氣：`would + 原形 ... if + 過去式(were)`；`if it weren't for ...` 是固定句型「要不是⋯⋯」。
- **`They have to come up with something equally good, but they only have two years to do it.`** — `come up with` 片語動詞（想出）＋ `something` 後置形容詞 `equally good`（不定代名詞的形容詞後置修飾）。

## 2. 從 Twitter 帳號談起：頭像裡的費曼是誰

### 逐句對照

**主持人:** I am looking right now, as a framework for starting this conversation, at your Twitter account. And that might seem like an odd place for me to start. People who listen to this show a lot will know that I don't often do that. But I want to know two things: you follow zero people on Twitter, yet you have almost a million followers. And also, trending right now in the United States is Jamie Foxx.
我現在正看著你的 Twitter 帳號，把它當作開啟這段對話的切入點。這聽起來可能是個奇怪的起點。常聽這個節目的人會知道我不常這樣做。但我想知道兩件事：你在 Twitter 上零追蹤，卻有將近一百萬名追蹤者。另外，現在在美國正熱搜的是 Jamie Foxx。

**Naval:** Hilarious. He's right next to you, he's reminding me. This cannot keep this guy down.
太好笑了。他就在你旁邊，一直提醒著我。這傢伙就是壓不下去。

**主持人:** So let's talk about your account. I find your account fascinating, I find your use of Twitter fascinating. And I'd like to begin with a photograph of a professor at a blackboard that you have as your profile header. Who is the person in the photograph? I think I recognize this person, but I want to hear you describe why you chose this photograph and who it is.
那我們來聊聊你的帳號。我覺得你的帳號很迷人，你使用 Twitter 的方式也很迷人。我想從一張照片開始：你的個人檔案頁首是一位教授站在黑板前。照片裡的人是誰？我想我認得這個人，但我想聽你描述為什麼選這張照片，以及他是誰。

**Naval:** Yeah, that's Professor Richard Feynman, who was a pioneer in the field of quantum electrodynamics. He was part of the Manhattan Project. He's known colloquially for his book Surely You're Joking, Mr. Feynman, and a couple of related ones like What Do You Care What Other People Think?
對，那是理查·費曼教授，他是量子電動力學領域的先驅。他參與過曼哈頓計畫。他在大眾間比較廣為人知的，是他那本書《別鬧了，費曼先生》，以及幾本相關的書，像是《你管別人怎麼想》。

**Naval:** But he's also very well regarded in the field of physics, obviously. He passed away a while back, but he invented a lot of quantum electrodynamics, Feynman path integrals. He was part of the Manhattan Project. Brilliant, brilliant guy.
不過顯然他在物理學界也非常受推崇。他很久以前就過世了，但他在量子電動力學上有許多創見，還有費曼路徑積分。他參與過曼哈頓計畫。是個非常非常聰明的人。

**Naval:** And I loved him because Feynman was one of the first characters that I encountered that did science and serious work and was accomplished in so-called real life, but was also like a character. He was a happy person, he was deeply philosophical, he didn't take himself nor life too seriously. He appreciated the mysteries of life, he appreciated living life, and he had a lot of fun along the way.
我喜歡他，是因為費曼是我最早遇到的那種人物之一：他做科學、做嚴肅的研究、在所謂的現實生活裡也很有成就，但同時又很有個性。他是個快樂的人、極富哲思，不把自己或人生看得太重。他欣賞生命的奧妙、享受活著這件事，而且一路上玩得很開心。

**Naval:** So to me he was like a full-stack intellectual hacker of life, and was very inspirational to me as a kid growing up. Even though I read a lot of philosophy and just kind of normal non-fiction, my heroes are scientists, even though I'm in the business world, the tech world.
所以對我來說，他就像一個全端的、對人生充滿智識的駭客，在我成長的過程中給了我很大的啟發。雖然我讀了很多哲學和一般的非虛構類書籍，但我的英雄是科學家，儘管我身處商業界、科技界。

**Naval:** Because I think applied science is the engine that pulls humanity forward. It eventually becomes technology, and that technology allows us to engage in all kinds of pursuits around civilization, rather than be scrapping for just basic substance and survival.
因為我認為應用科學是拉動人類前進的引擎。它最終會變成技術，而那些技術讓我們能投入文明中各式各樣的追求，而不是只為了最基本的溫飽和生存而掙扎。

**Naval:** And so I think scientists are still the most unsung heroes of human history, and Feynman was one of the greats. He was admirable in many regards as to how he lived his life. Although I think recently they tried to cancel him, but they try to cancel everybody.
所以我認為科學家仍然是人類歷史上最被埋沒的英雄，而費曼是其中的佼佼者。他在很多方面都活得令人欽佩。雖然最近好像有人想「取消」他，但他們什麼人都想取消。

**主持人:** Feynman is a character, certainly. He is a character, was a character, and a figure who has fascinated me for decades.
費曼確實是個有個性的人物。他現在是、過去也是個有意思的角色，一個讓我著迷了數十年的人物。

### 💬 慣用語 & 口語解析

- **`right next to you`**：「就在你旁邊」，此處帶戲謔——指 Jamie Foxx 又在熱搜上緊咬著。
- **`keep this guy down`**：「壓制住這傢伙」；`cannot keep him down` 意為「怎麼也壓不下去」。
- **`a full-stack ... hacker of life`**：借用工程術語 `full-stack`（全端），形容費曼是「對人生全方位通吃」的人。
- **`along the way`**：「一路上、在過程中」。
- **`cancel someone`**：現代網路用語「取消某人」，指群起抵制、封殺某公眾人物。

### 📝 文法 / 句型註記

- **`He didn't take himself nor life too seriously.`** — `take ... seriously`（把⋯⋯當回事）＋反身代名詞 `himself`；此處 `nor` 連接兩個受詞，屬口語用法（較正式應為 `or`）。
- **`the most unsung heroes of human history`** — 最高級 `the most + 形容詞`；`unsung`（未被歌頌的）是常與 `hero` 搭配的固定用語。

## 3. 收藏費曼手稿與「科學是對專家無知的信念」（可證偽性）

### 逐句對照

**主持人:** And actually I collect very few things, but managed to buy some of his papers and drawings at auction a handful of years ago, because it was the first time that they had been made available. Hey, I can show you some of his equations from the Manhattan Project, which is wild to think about.
其實我收藏的東西很少，但幾年前設法在拍賣會上買到了一些他的手稿和圖畫，因為那是它們第一次公開釋出。嘿，我可以給你看看他在曼哈頓計畫時期寫的一些方程式，光是想到就覺得很不可思議。

**主持人:** And my fascination, though, is in many respects uninformed, because I don't have the scientific literacy to fully appreciate the genius that he brought to bear on physics, for instance. But I could appreciate his cleverness and his questioning of assumptions, and also his distaste for uniforms and other accolades, most notably some of the prizes that he was awarded that he seemed to have a particular dislike for.
不過我的著迷，在許多方面其實是外行的，因為我沒有足夠的科學素養去完全領會他在物理上所展現的那份天才。但我能欣賞他的機智、他對各種假設的質疑，還有他對制式榮譽和獎項的反感——最明顯的就是他所獲頒的一些獎項，他似乎對那些特別反感。

**Naval:** He famously said that science is the belief in the ignorance of experts. And it's so funny because these days so many people are on Twitter saying "believe in science" or "according to science," and you have to realize there's no such thing as science with a capital S.
他有句名言：科學就是對專家無知的信念。這很好笑，因為這年頭這麼多人在 Twitter 上說「相信科學」或「根據科學」，可是你得明白，根本沒有大寫 S 的「科學」這種東西。

**Naval:** The foundation of science is doubt. It is all about falsifiability. David Deutsch, who's kind of one of my current modern living physicist heroes, is a Popperian. He subscribes to Karl Popper's philosophy, and he basically says that if it's not falsifiable, it's not scientific.
科學的根基是懷疑。它的核心是可證偽性。大衛·多伊奇是我目前在世的現代物理學家英雄之一，他是波普爾主義者。他認同卡爾·波普爾的哲學，基本上他說：如果一件事無法被證偽，它就不算科學。

**Naval:** If you can't prove it false, if it's true up or down or left or right, it's not science. It doesn't require belief. You should be able to challenge it at all times. It should make risky predictions that are not obvious, and those predictions should then be tested.
如果你無法證明它是錯的，如果它不管上下左右怎麼看都成立，那它就不是科學。它不需要信仰。你應該隨時都能挑戰它。它應該做出不顯而易見的、有風險的預測，然後這些預測要拿去接受檢驗。

**Naval:** And you shouldn't be able to, after the fact, change the goalpost, or change how the predictions were set up. So those are the hallmarks of good science: falsifiability, independent verifiability, and making risky and narrow predictions with details that are hard to vary, both before and after the fact.
而且你不該在事後移動球門柱，或更動預測當初是怎麼設定的。所以這些就是好科學的標誌：可證偽性、可獨立驗證，以及做出風險高、範圍窄、細節難以更動的預測——事前事後都是如此。

**Naval:** And that's how you end up with good science and good explanations, not by people on Twitter voting. That's politics. So like everything has become politics now, science has become politics too. People are politicized, and there's big science. But I think Feynman would be very unhappy with that.
這才是你得到好科學、好解釋的方式，而不是靠一群人在 Twitter 上投票。那是政治。就像現在一切都變成政治，科學也變成政治了。人們被政治化，還出現了「大科學」。但我想費曼會對此非常不滿。

**Naval:** He was just a generally happy character and non-political, intended not to get involved in these things. But I think he would realize that that is not actually science, it's masquerading as science. It's almost becoming social science.
他基本上是個快樂、不涉政的人，刻意不捲入這些事情。但我想他會意識到，那其實不是科學，只是打著科學的幌子。它幾乎快變成社會科學了。

### 💬 慣用語 & 口語解析

- **`bring to bear on`**：「施加、投入（能力／力量）於某事」。例：`the genius that he brought to bear on physics`。
- **`science with a capital S`**：「大寫 S 的科學」，諷刺被神化、當口號喊的「科學」。
- **`after the fact`**：「事後、事情發生之後」。
- **`move the goalpost`**：「移動球門柱」，比喻中途更改標準／目標讓人達不到。
- **`masquerade as`**：「假扮成、冒充為」。

### 📝 文法 / 句型註記

- **`If you can't prove it false, ... it's not science.`** — 第一類條件句（真實條件）：`if + 現在式, 主句現在式`；`prove it false` 為「受詞 + 受詞補語」結構。
- **`it was the first time that they had been made available`** — `the first time that ...` 後接過去完成式 `had been made`，表示比主要過去時間更早發生的動作，並用被動語態。

## 4. 塔雷伯與社會科學：個人追求真理、群體追求共識

### 逐句對照

**Naval:** And going to Twitter, like Nassim Taleb is one of my favorite Twitter follows, even though he's a bit cantankerous.
說到 Twitter，像納西姆·塔雷伯就是我最愛追蹤的帳號之一，雖然他有點難搞、脾氣壞。

**主持人:** Yeah, he's cantankerous, yeah, he annoys people for sure.
對，他很暴躁，是啊，他肯定很惹人厭。

**Naval:** But you want to hear the truth, you gotta be prepared to be hurt a little bit. But he had a great tweet recently that I retweeted, which I'm sure made a lot of people mad, where he said the opposite of education is not ignorance, the opposite of education is an education in social science. And I thought that was hilarious.
但你要想聽真話，就得準備好被刺傷一點。他最近發了一則很棒的推文，我轉推了，我相信那讓很多人氣炸——他說：教育的反面不是無知，教育的反面是接受社會科學的教育。我覺得這太好笑了。

- **`make money off (of) something`**：「靠某物賺錢、從中獲利」。
- **`turn of phrase`**：「一種措辭、遣詞用字」，常指說得巧妙的表達。
- **`the interwebs`**：`internet` 的戲謔說法，帶輕鬆自嘲的網路口吻。
- **`go get it`**：口語激勵語，「去把它拿下、去爭取吧」。

### 📝 文法 / 句型註記

- **`It was very nice of him`** — `It is + adj. + of + 人 (+ to V)` 評價某人行為的句型，「某人這麼做真是⋯⋯」。
- **`don't be too hard on that roommate in your head`** — `be hard on someone` 表「對某人嚴厲／苛刻」；此處以「腦中的室友」比喻內在的自我批判聲音。
