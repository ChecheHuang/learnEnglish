## 1. 開場：現在還能「一直買進」嗎？

### 逐句對照

**主持人:** In this market, can a normal investor like me just keep buying?
在這樣的市場裡，像我這樣的一般投資人還能一直買進嗎？

**Nick:** You know what? This seems too crazy. I even got a little pessimistic at one point.
你知道嗎？這一切看起來太瘋狂了。我甚至一度變得有點悲觀。

**主持人:** Wow, that's new.
哇，這倒是新鮮事。

**Nick:** Depends on your time frame. That's everything.
這要看你的時間框架（time frame）。這才是關鍵。

**Nick:** Like if you need the money in the next 5 years, no. I would say absolutely not. You should not be buying right now.
如果你在未來五年內就需要用到這筆錢，那不行。我會說絕對不要。你現在不該進場買。

**Nick:** If this is a retirement account for 20, 30 years in the future, I would say yes, cuz the market will surprise you in many ways.
但如果這是一個為了二、三十年後準備的退休帳戶，我會說可以，因為市場會在許多方面讓你大吃一驚。

### 💬 慣用語 & 口語解析

- **`You know what?`**：「你知道嗎？」用來開啟一段話、引起對方注意的口語起手式。
- **`That's new.`**：「這倒是新鮮事／這是頭一遭。」帶點驚訝或調侃的回應。
- **`That's everything.`**：「這就是一切／這才是最關鍵的。」強調某件事的決定性。
- **`cuz`**：`because` 的口語縮寫（唸 /kʌz/），逐字稿常見。

### 📝 文法 / 句型註記

- **`Depends on your time frame.`** — 口語中省略主詞 `It`，完整為 `It depends on...`。`depend on + 名詞` 表「取決於⋯⋯」。
- **`if you need the money in the next 5 years, no`** — 條件句省略主句，只用 `no` 回答，是口語常見的簡答方式。

## 2. 一年前看空、現在看多：AI 是過度投資還是典範轉移？

### 逐句對照

**Nick:** A year ago I was bearish. I'm now bullish once again.
一年前我看空（bearish），現在我又重新看多（bullish）了。

**Nick:** Over the last year, because AI was seen so crazy, I got a little bearish for the first time ever, and I reduced my stock exposure by 20%.
過去這一年，因為 AI 熱到太誇張，我這輩子頭一次變得有點看空，把我的股票部位（stock exposure）減碼了 20%。

**Nick:** I went from 100% stocks in my retirement account to 80/20, which to me is like being bearish. And at this point now I'm like, why did I do that?
我把退休帳戶從 100% 全押股票，調成 80/20，這對我來說就等於是看空了。而現在的我心想，我當初幹嘛那樣做？

**Nick:** It's probably a bit of both, right? If you look at the history of any large technological change, there's always a ton of players that rush in and there's a lot of capital that goes in.
大概兩者都有一點吧？如果你回顧任何一次重大技術變革的歷史，總是會有一大堆玩家搶著進場，也會有大量資金湧入。

**Nick:** There's always over-investment, and I guarantee in this case there's going to be some over-investment. Whether it's already happened, whether it's going to happen in the future, I don't know. We can't predict the future.
過度投資（over-investment）永遠都會發生，我敢保證這一次也一定會有一些過度投資。至於是已經發生了、還是未來才會發生，我不知道。我們沒辦法預測未來。

**Nick:** At the same time, it is a paradigm shift for a lot of things. Anyone that's used ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, you name it, you've seen things like, wow, this can save me time.
但同時，這對很多事情來說確實是一場典範轉移（paradigm shift）。任何用過 ChatGPT、Claude、Perplexity，隨便你舉的，都會有那種「哇，這能幫我省時間」的感受。

**Nick:** There's now bots out there that can do things, and they're not perfect, but they can save a lot of time.
現在市面上已經有能實際做事的機器人（bot），它們不完美，但確實能省下很多時間。

**Nick:** It's a question of, like, the economics haven't fully worn out yet. Cuz at the end of the day, AI could be just as good as a human, but what if it's more expensive than a human?
問題在於，這門生意的經濟帳（the economics）還沒完全算清楚。因為說到底，AI 可能跟人一樣厲害，但萬一它比請一個人還貴呢？

**Nick:** Everyone thinks the cost of compute is going to keep coming down to the point where it's cheaper than people. And if that's the case, then that's great, but there's a lot of things we have to think through there.
大家都認為運算成本（cost of compute）會一直降，降到比請人還便宜。如果真是這樣，那很好，但這中間有很多事情我們得想清楚。

**Nick:** It's a very complex question. So overall I would say, yes, there's over-investment, but also we know it's a paradigm shift, so it's both. And that's why it's so confusing.
這是個非常複雜的問題。所以整體來說我會講，是的，有過度投資，但我們也知道這是一場典範轉移，所以兩者皆是。這正是它讓人如此困惑的原因。

**Nick:** If it was an obvious answer, we wouldn't be having this debate right now.
如果這題答案很明顯，我們現在根本不會在這裡爭論。

## 3. 為什麼你轉為看空？股價營收比與 2021 既視感

### 逐句對照

**主持人:** Okay, that's really interesting cuz you turned a little bit bearish. So based on what? Is that the earnings or the sentiment?
好，這很有意思，因為你當時轉為有點看空。那是基於什麼呢？是因為財報獲利（earnings），還是市場情緒（sentiment）？

**Nick:** Price to sales. There were too many things that just reminded me of 2021, and I think I got a little bit of déjà vu.
股價營收比（price to sales）。有太多事情都讓我想起 2021 年，我覺得自己有點既視感（déjà vu）。

**Nick:** Like Chamath was coming out with SPACs again, like I was seeing price to earnings. I overlaid the Nvidia price to sales with the Microsoft price to sales in '99, and they matched.
像是 Chamath 又開始搞 SPAC（特殊目的收購公司），我又看到那種本益比。我把 Nvidia 的股價營收比疊在微軟 1999 年的股價營收比上，結果兩條線幾乎吻合。

**Nick:** Of course, there's a lot of coincidences. I was like, this is just too crazy.
當然啦，這裡面有很多巧合。我那時心想，這實在太瘋狂了。

**Nick:** I like that metric more than earnings, cuz earnings can be manipulated with all sorts of accounting tricks. Sales is like, that's what you have.
比起獲利，我更喜歡股價營收比這個指標，因為獲利可以用各種會計手法動手腳。營收就是實打實的，那就是你真正有的東西。

**Nick:** You have the price of your company and you have your revenue. To fake sales is like straight-up fraud, and people very rarely do that. People can play accounting tricks with earnings; you can't do that with sales.
你有公司的股價，你也有你的營收（revenue）。要造假營收根本就是徹頭徹尾的詐欺，很少人會這麼幹。獲利可以玩會計花招，但營收沒辦法這樣搞。

**Nick:** So I was looking at these data series and I was like, this seems crazier than the dot-com bubble.
所以我看著這些數據序列，心想，這看起來比網路泡沫（dot-com bubble）還要瘋狂。

**Nick:** There's just a lot of growth, and that maybe justified it, cuz a lot of that growth has happened. I mean, look at what happened with Anthropic.
只是這裡面有非常大的成長，而這或許讓高估值變得合理，因為那些成長很多都真的實現了。我是說，你看 Anthropic 發生了什麼。

**Nick:** I think I just read they were 3 billion ARR, now they're 45 billion ARR in a year. That is absurd growth.
我記得我剛讀到，他們的年度經常性收入（ARR）本來是 30 億美元，一年內就變成 450 億美元。這成長誇張到不行。

**Nick:** So the growth is coming, not for every company, but for a lot of them. And so maybe I was too bearish, because I just thought the growth expectations were crazy, and they've happened.
所以成長真的來了，不是每一家公司，但很多家都是。所以也許我當時太看空了，因為我只覺得那些成長預期太離譜，結果它們竟然都兌現了。

**Nick:** The growth has come, and so it's interesting whether it'll keep coming. That's a second debate to have.
成長已經來了，所以接下來會不會繼續來，就很值得玩味了。那是另一個要另外辯的題目。

**Nick:** But I think that's why I got bearish, cuz I just couldn't imagine a scenario where a company would go from 3 billion ARR to 45 billion in a year, or anything even close to that, and they've easily done that.
但我想這就是我轉為看空的原因，因為我實在無法想像有哪家公司能在一年內從 30 億 ARR 衝到 450 億，或哪怕接近這數字，而他們卻輕輕鬆鬆就做到了。

**Nick:** And so that's just an example of the type of stuff that's happening in these markets.
所以這就是現在這些市場裡正在發生的那類事情的一個例子。

## 4. 從看空到看多是「慢慢意識到的」／要凌駕情緒

### 逐句對照

**主持人:** Do you still remember the moment you changed from being bearish to bullish, and why?
你還記得你從看空轉為看多的那一刻嗎？還有為什麼？

**Nick:** I don't think there's one moment. I think it's a slow realization of these things happening.
我不覺得有哪個特定的一刻。我想這比較像是慢慢意識到（a slow realization）這些事情正在發生。

**Nick:** I was looking at the data saying, "Hey, this looks like this is the case. This can't keep going." And then a lot of them hit those targets. Earnings keep going up.
我看著數據，心想：「欸，看起來就是這麼回事，這不可能一直漲下去。」結果很多公司達成了那些目標。獲利一直往上衝。

**Nick:** A lot of these things I didn't think were going to happen ended up happening, and so I was wrong. And it happens, you know?
很多我原本以為不會發生的事，最後都發生了，所以我錯了。這種事難免嘛，你懂的。

**Nick:** That's why, it seems so stupid to say this, but you have to ignore all of the stuff that's coming out and just, what does history say? And history says just keep buying.
這就是為什麼——講出來好像很蠢——你必須無視外頭所有冒出來的雜訊，只問：歷史怎麼說？而歷史說的是：持續買進（just keep buying）。

**Nick:** I've written about it, I have a book on it, and even I went against that because of what was happening in the moment. And so that's where you have to override your emotion.
我寫過這件事，我還為它寫了一本書，可是連我自己都因為當下正在發生的事而違背了它。所以這正是你必須凌駕自己情緒（override your emotion）的地方。

**Nick:** Now, I didn't do anything crazy. No matter what, even if this market was even crazier than dot-com, I would still be buying US stocks. I would just reduce how much I'm buying.
不過，我並沒有做什麼瘋狂的事。無論如何，就算這個市場比網路泡沫還瘋，我還是會買美股。我只是會減少買進的金額。

**Nick:** So for me it's like, I'm always buying US stocks. It's just a question of how much.
所以對我來說就是，我永遠都在買美股。差別只在於買多少。

**Nick:** Instead of going 100% into equities, which is developed world international plus US equities, I said, "Okay, I'm going to only do 80% into those." So I just dropped my exposure a little bit and kept buying from there.
我沒有把 100% 全押到股票——也就是已開發國家的國際股加上美股——而是說：「好，我只放 80% 進去。」所以我只是把部位稍微降一點，然後就從那裡繼續買。

**Nick:** So I always make micro shifts when I get, quote, bearish, but I'm never bearish enough to be like, "Oh, I'm selling out of all my stocks."
所以每當我變得所謂「看空」時，我永遠只做微幅調整（micro shifts），但我絕不會空到說：「喔，我要把手上所有股票全賣光。」

**Nick:** I would never do something like that, cuz the data is overwhelmingly positive. You should never do something like that.
我絕對不會做那種事，因為數據壓倒性地正面。你也永遠不該做那種事。

## 5. 全書核心咒語：持續買進一籃子生息資產

### 逐句對照

**Nick:** The mantra of the book is: if I can only give you one line, one piece of investment advice, it would be this. The continual purchase of a diverse set of income-producing assets.
這本書的核心咒語（mantra）是：如果我只能給你一句話、一條投資建議，那就是這句——持續買進一組多元的生息資產（income-producing assets）。

**Nick:** There's three pillars to that. Continual purchase: you're buying over time. And I show in the data why this is true.
這句話有三根支柱（pillars）。「持續買進」：你是隨著時間一直買。而我用數據證明了為什麼這是對的。

**Nick:** Even if I gave you information about the future, you would be better off just buying every month. There are chapters that cover that.
就算我把未來的資訊給你，你還是每個月固定買進會比較划算。書裡有專門的章節在講這件事。

- **`look forward to + N/Ving`**：「期待⋯⋯」，注意 to 是介系詞，後接名詞或動名詞。
- **`DM me`**：「私訊我」，DM（direct message）在社群語境中常直接當動詞用。
- **`have someone on`**：「邀某人上（節目）」的片語動詞。
- **`it's all good`**：「都沒問題、都行」，輕鬆表示不必客氣、不用擔心。

### 📝 文法 / 句型註記

- **`thank you for having me on`** — `thank you for + Ving` 表達感謝的固定句型；`have someone on` 為「邀某人上節目」的片語動詞。
- **`feel free to just use a translator if you don't speak English`** — `feel free to + V` 表「儘管去做、別客氣」；後接 if 條件子句補述適用情境。
